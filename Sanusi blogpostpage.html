How I Built a Traffic Light System Using a 4017

Key Steps

In this post, I’m excited to share my journey of building a simple traffic light circuit using a 555 timer and a CD4017 decade counter:

  • I started by learning how the 555 timer produces clock pulses, which was fascinating!
  • Next, I connected the CD4017 to manage the sequence of the LEDs, which was like putting together a puzzle.
  • After that, I tested the timing for the red, yellow, and green lights to ensure everything was working smoothly.
  • Finally, I assembled the circuit on a breadboard and observed the output with great excitement.

Conclusion: This project not only deepened my understanding of timing control and digital logic but also sparked my interest in embedded systems design.

My Experience During SIWES

In this post, I want to share my incredible experience during the Student Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES):

  • I honed my soldering and desoldering skills on actual circuit boards, which was both challenging and rewarding.
  • Through hands-on practice, I learned to identify components like resistors, capacitors, and ICs—things I had only read about.
  • I got to work on assembling regulated power supply circuits, which gave me a real feel for practical electronics.
  • It was enlightening to see how what I learned in class applied to real-world engineering tasks.

Conclusion: My time during SIWES significantly boosted my confidence in circuit design and reinforced my academic knowledge with practical experience.
